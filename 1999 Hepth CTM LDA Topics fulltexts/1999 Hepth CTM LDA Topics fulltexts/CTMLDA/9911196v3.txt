i. introduction

the renormalization group (rg) method has proved one of the most important tools
in rened perturbative analysis. the concept of the rg-improved perturbation theory was
originally introduced long ago within the context of quantum electrodynamics (qed) in the
landmark work of gell-mann and low [1]. in the expression of an rg-improved quantity,
whether it be the greens function, the eective potential, or any other quantity predictable
from feynman diagram perturbation theory, the bare parameters in the corresponding ex-
pression are replaced with their scale-dependent running forms which are usually calculated
to some given order in the perturbation theory.

in one of the early applications of the rg method, coleman and weinberg [2] considered
the eective potential v () for a spacetime-independent scalar eld  in the context of
massless models. in the massive case, it has been demonstrated that this treatment also
works provided one takes into account a nontrivial running of the vacuum energy [36].1

in this paper, we extend the earlier work of the present authors [8], in which the next-
next-to-leading logarithm resummation of the eective potential for a single-component mas-
sive 4 theory was obtained, to the next-next-next-to-leading logarithm order. in sec. ii,
without discussing the technical details of how the eective potential is computed, a sum-
mary of the ms three-loop eective potential is given. in sec. iii, the perturbation solutions
for the running parameters (t), m2(t), (t), and (t) are obtained, and the result for the
next-next-next-to-leading logarithm resummation is reported. the nal section is devoted
to concluding remarks. in the appendix, we quote the previous result given in ref. 8 for the
next-next-to-leading logarithm resummation of the eective potential.
